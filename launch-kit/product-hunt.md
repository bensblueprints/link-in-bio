## Full description

Link-in-Bio is a self-hosted link-in-bio page builder for people who are done renting their own audience page.

**What you get:**
- A fast, server-rendered public page at your own domain — mobile-first, SEO + OG tags, zero external requests (your visitors are never pinged by Google Fonts or anyone else)
- Link blocks with thumbnails and hover animations, header dividers, YouTube embeds
- An email-collect block that stores subscribers in *your* SQLite database, with one-click CSV export
- 6 polished themes (gradient, glass, minimal, dark, neon, paper) + custom accent, custom backgrounds, a curated font picker, and a raw custom-CSS box
- Click tracking, page views, CTR per link, and a 30-day chart — analytics that live on your disk, not someone's dashboard
- Link scheduling with start/end dates
- Drag-to-reorder admin with a clean dark UI

**Two ways to run it:** double-click it as a desktop app (Electron), or `docker compose up` on a $5 VPS when you want it public.

Source is MIT on GitHub. The paid version is the 1-click installer for people who don't want to touch a terminal — pay once, own it forever.

## Maker first comment

Hey PH 👋

I built this because I got tired of paying Linktree $9/month for what is, honestly, one HTML page. Then a musician friend got her page rate-limited during a release week and I realized the deeper problem: your link-in-bio is often your single most valuable URL, and you don't own it.

So Link-in-Bio is the whole thing, self-hosted: your domain, your themes, your click data, your email list in a SQLite file you can literally copy to a USB stick. The public page is server-rendered HTML with zero external requests — it loads instantly on a phone on bad festival wifi, which is exactly where fans click these links.

It's MIT on GitHub if you want to build it yourself. The $19 version is for creators who'd rather click an installer than open a terminal — that's the entire business model, no subscription, no upsell tiers.

Honest limitations: it's single-profile (one page per install), and email capture stores + exports CSV — it doesn't send campaigns. Happy to answer anything!
